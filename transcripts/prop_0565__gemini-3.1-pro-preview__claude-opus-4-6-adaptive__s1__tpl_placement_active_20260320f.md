[C1.3] Placing this expensive, probabilistic intervention into basic coverage necessarily diverts limited funds from treatments that prevent death and disability. [C1.4] The WHO classifies infertility as a disease, but classification alone cannot determine coverage priority. Mild androgenetic hair loss is a medical condition too. Redirecting public money toward a procedure that overwhelmingly benefits middle-class couples in their thirties—the demographic most likely to seek IVF—while wait times lengthen for surgeries and psychiatric beds remain scarce is regressive, not progressive.
