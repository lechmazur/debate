[P1.1] Mandatory retirement is a blunt, outdated instrument that fundamentally contradicts the principle of merit. By forcing individuals out of the workforce at an arbitrary age, we substitute a birth date for an actual assessment of capability, effectively institutionalizing age discrimination. This practice must be abolished in most occupations because it is unjust to workers, economically self-destructive, and entirely unnecessary given modern workforce management.
[P1.2] First, chronological age is a notoriously poor proxy for competence. Cognitive and physical decline happen at vastly different rates. By enforcing a universal expiration date, employers discard highly capable, experienced professionals while protecting younger, potentially less competent workers from fair competition. If an occupation requires specific physical or cognitive standards, employers should test for those exact standards.
[P1.3] Objective performance evaluations, regular medical clearances, and practical skills tests evaluate actual fitness to work far better than a calendar does. Firing someone merely because they turned 65, regardless of their stellar performance, is an indefensible violation of their right to be judged on merit.
[P1.4] Second, mandatory retirement is economically disastrous in the face of modern demographic shifts. With increasing life expectancies and declining birth rates, economies face severe labor shortages and unsustainable strains on pension systems. Forcing productive, willing citizens out of the workforce actively exacerbates this crisis. We are draining our industries of invaluable institutional knowledge and forcing society to support people who would otherwise be generating wealth and tax revenue.
[P1.5] Finally, forced retirement inflicts profound personal harm. As people live longer, many simply cannot afford to retire at a legacy statutory age due to inflation and inadequate savings. Denying them the right to work manufactures financial precariousness. Furthermore, abrupt removal from the workforce strips many of their primary source of community and purpose, which frequently accelerates cognitive and physical decline.
[P1.6] We have the human resources tools to evaluate workers individually and fairly. It is time to abandon the lazy, discriminatory shortcut of mandatory retirement and embrace a labor market defined by actual ability rather than age.
