[P1.5] These achievements required no life support, no return trajectory, no emergency abort systems. When the mission ends, the hardware stays. The science does not.
[P1.6] Crewed missions carry a cost structure that grows with every safety requirement added to protect human life. Every dollar spent keeping an astronaut alive is a dollar not pointed at a detector, a spectrograph, or a relay transmitter.
[P1.7] The proposition does not ask whether humans should ever go to space. It asks whether most exploration budgets should shift toward what actually maximizes scientific return.
