[P1.3] Markets cannot discipline such sellers. The ordinary price-and-choice mechanism that works for sneakers or streaming services collapses when the buyer is an 87-year-old with dementia whose children are desperate and exhausted.

Regulation of ownership structure is not new; we already bar certain investors from prisons, media outlets, and banks for exactly the same reason of misaligned incentives.
[P1.5] The central clash is therefore clear: one side trusts financial engineers to self-correct when lives are at stake; the other recognizes that elder care is a fiduciary domain where profit maximization must be bounded. The alternative is to accept preventable suffering as an acceptable cost of capital allocation. That is indefensible.
