[C1.1] The proposition's case rests on a seductive logic: broken market, therefore subsidy, therefore problem solved. But the motion isn't simply "use AMCs." It says permit AMCs *even if unit prices remain high*. That conditional is doing enormous work, and it's precisely where the proposition fails.
[C1.2] High unit prices aren't incidental. They are the mechanism through which wealth flows from public health budgets into pharmaceutical revenue. When a government commits in advance to purchase a drug at an elevated price, it hands a company guaranteed income regardless of whether the drug is actually deployed widely. That's not incentive alignment—it's a blank check with antibiotic branding on it.
[C1.3] The stewardship problem is the first crack. New antibiotics should be held in reserve—used sparingly—to extend their useful lifespan. But AMC structures that depend on volume create pressure, subtle or explicit, to prescribe these drugs more broadly to justify the expenditure. If a country has committed to purchasing a substantial stockpile at high unit cost, that stock becomes a sunk cost demanding utilization.
[C1.4] This directly undermines resistance management, the very problem we're supposedly solving.
[C1.5] The second crack is opportunity cost. Health budgets are finite. Every dollar committed under an AMC at elevated prices is a dollar unavailable for sanitation improvements, infection prevention, and stewardship programs that reduce resistance pressure today without waiting a decade for a new drug to emerge. The proposition frames the alternative as "no new antibiotics"—a false binary.
[C1.6] The real alternative includes better-designed pull incentives that delink payment from unit price entirely, eliminating the perverse pressures high prices create.
[C1.7] Finally, the proposition's "tiered pricing" fix is aspirational, not structural. Nothing in an AMC mandate requires affordability guarantees to hold once the commitment period ends. Countries get a drug they've overpaid to develop, then face monopoly pricing afterward.
[C1.8] The motion as written entrenches the wrong incentive at exactly the wrong point.
