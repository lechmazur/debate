[P1.1] Three reasons compel a ban on psychological micro-targeting in political campaigns.
[P1.2] **First: it attacks the precondition of democratic consent.** Ordinary persuasion treats voters as reasoning agents — you make an argument in public, others can hear it, contest it, fact-check it. Psychological micro-targeting does the opposite. A campaign infers that you are anxious, lonely, conscientious, or prone to disgust, and then tailors a message engineered to bypass deliberation and pull a specific lever in your limbic system. The voter never sees the pitch sent to her neighbor.
[P1.3] Journalists cannot audit it. Opponents cannot rebut it. This is not speech in the public square; it is a thousand whispered, unfalsifiable pitches calibrated to private vulnerabilities. Democracy can survive lies — it has tools for those.
[P1.4] It cannot survive the systematic destruction of the shared informational environment in which lies get caught.
[P1.5] **Second: the inference itself is the harm.** The CON side will say "just regulate misuse." But the practice requires building, buying, or renting psychographic dossiers — guesses about your mental health, trauma triggers, susceptibility to fear appeals — assembled without your knowledge from data you surrendered for unrelated purposes. Even if every ad were truthful, the infrastructure is a standing weapon. The Cambridge Analytica episode, whatever one believes about its actual efficacy, showed how casually such profiles get assembled and traded.
[P1.6] Banning the campaign use cuts demand for that infrastructure at the root.
[P1.7] **Third: it is uniquely exploitative in the political context.** We already restrict targeting depressed people with gambling ads or children with tobacco ads, because emotional exploitation of a vulnerable state is wrong even when the underlying product is legal. A vote is more consequential than either. A campaign identifying voters scoring high on neuroticism and feeding them custom fear narratives is doing exactly what we forbid lesser industries from doing.
[P1.8] The motion does not ban political speech, persuasion, demographic outreach, or issue ads. It bans weaponizing inferred psychological profiles. That is the right line, and democracy needs it drawn.
