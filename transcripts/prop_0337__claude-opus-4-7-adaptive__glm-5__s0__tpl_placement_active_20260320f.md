[P1.5] Defaults matter precisely because attention-capturing products exploit inertia; using that same inertia to protect kids is the minimum coherent response. [P1.6] Third, the engagement objection is a category error. "Engagement falls" is a cost to the platform, not to the child. What falls is the compulsive overhang—the hour-three and hour-four of a session no teenager endorses on reflection.
[P1.7] So the clash should be judged on a simple comparison: on one side, measurable risks to sleep, mental health, and attention in a developing brain; on the other, a revenue line on a quarterly deck.
