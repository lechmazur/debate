[P1.1] The core question here is deceptively simple: what actually fills the hole? [P1.3] Start with mechanism. A literacy campaign teaches a sixteen-year-old to recognize a manipulative feed, to pause before sharing, to question what she reads. That is genuinely useful. [P1.5] The violence link follows the same logic. Most youth violence research points to the same cluster of risk factors: social marginalization, unsupervised hours, lack of future-oriented identity, absence of trusted non-parental adults. [P1.6] The policy stakes make this concrete. Governments have finite budgets, and every dollar spent on a literacy campaign is a dollar not available for the coach, the rehearsal space, the equipment subsidy that lets a kid from a low-income family actually participate. When we compare these two tools, we are implicitly comparing two theories of what makes young people safer and less alone. One theory says information changes behavior.
