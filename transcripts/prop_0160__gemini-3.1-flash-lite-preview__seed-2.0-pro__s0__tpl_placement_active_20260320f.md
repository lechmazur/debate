A minimally trained conscript cannot stop a missile strike, and diverting money from the tools that can is not risk mitigation—it is self-sabotage.
[C2.2] The argument that conscripts fill critical logistical and civil defense gaps ignores that voluntary civil defense and reserve programs already do this work far more effectively. Volunteers choose to participate, receive ongoing targeted training instead of a single six-month block they largely forget within two years, and cost a fraction of what a universal forced program costs. Forcing disinterested 18-year-olds to sort disaster supplies for three months does not build a more resilient rear guard—it builds a less reliable one.
[C2.3] The claim that unfair exemptions are a fixable policy flaw ignores the inherent structural bias of every mandatory service system ever implemented. This is not a historical anomaly—it is a predictable feature of forcing service onto a population with wildly unequal access to resources, and it will deepen, not reduce, social resentment.
