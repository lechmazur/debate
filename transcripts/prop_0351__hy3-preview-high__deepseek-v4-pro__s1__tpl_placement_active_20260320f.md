Managers consistently overrate the performance of in-office juniors even when output is identical, because proximity breeds familiarity and casual trust.
[P1.4] Employers claim their evaluation processes are objective, but they rarely acknowledge this proximity bias, and juniors are loath to point it out for fear of seeming difficult or out of touch.
