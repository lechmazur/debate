[P1.1] NDAs in workplace harassment, discrimination, and abuse cases are not tools of privacy or mutual agreement—they are weapons of coercion that let bad actors evade accountability. Most victims of workplace misconduct face steep power imbalances: the employer holds the purse strings, controls the investigation, and can drag out litigation indefinitely. A common pattern sees a victim of sexual harassment by a direct supervisor offered a modest settlement only if they sign an NDA barring any discussion of the incident, the abuser’s identity, or even the existence of a payout.
[P1.2] The victim, often unable to afford months of unpaid legal battles, signs under duress. The abuser stays in their role, free to target more staff, while the employer avoids reputational damage and regulatory scrutiny.
[P1.3] Unenforceability of these NDAs would end this cycle without harming legitimate business interests. This proposition applies only to cases involving proven or alleged harassment, discrimination, or abuse, leaving standard NDAs for trade secrets, client data, and proprietary processes fully intact. If these gag clauses are unenforceable, employers can no longer bury patterns of misconduct. Victims retain the option to stay private if they wish, but are no longer legally barred from warning colleagues, reporting to regulators, or speaking out publicly.
[P1.4] Employers would be forced to address underlying misconduct instead of paying hush money, since the legal shield of silence is stripped away. Public awareness of repeat misconduct also helps regulators target bad actors faster, reducing harm across entire industries.
[P1.5] Opponents will claim this violates victim privacy, but that is a deliberate misdirection. Unenforceability does not force victims to speak—it only removes the employer’s power to legally silence them. The current system prioritizes the abuser’s and employer’s interests over the victim’s; this proposal flips that, putting agency back in the hands of the people most harmed, where it always should have been.
