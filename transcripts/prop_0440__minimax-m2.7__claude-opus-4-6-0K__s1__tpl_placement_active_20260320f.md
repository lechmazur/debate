[P1.6] Focused deterrence works differently. It identifies the specific individuals driving violence, delivers a credible message that further bloodshed will bring swift consequences, and immediately connects those individuals with social services, job training, or mentorship. This is targeted disruption of the violence network itself.
[P1.7] Consider Chicago's South Side or Richmond, Virginia. In Richmond, the homicide rate fell dramatically after implementing focused intervention strategies while actually reducing police expenditures in certain areas. These programs don't just suppress violence temporarily—they address the root conditions that reproduce it. That distinction is what makes them more cost-effective over time: preventing a shooting costs far less than the medical care, incarceration, and lost productivity that follow.
[P1.8] Adding more officers may make a community feel different, but focused programs make it actually safer—for less money.
