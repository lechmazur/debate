[P1.3] Antitrust law has long recognized that coordination need not involve explicit meetings or phone calls to be illegal. Landlords retain the theoretical freedom to ignore the recommendation, but the financial pressure to comply, combined with the tool’s market-wide visibility, creates a self-enforcing pricing discipline.

[C1.5] The same logic applies to landlords.

[C1.7] Current doctrine already provides adequate protection. We do not need to stretch the law to cover independent tool use; we merely need to enforce existing standards against genuine conspiracies.
