By the time a studio decides to use an actor’s voice for a different language dub or an entirely new performance, the actor has lost all leverage.

This creates a fair market where actors are compensated for the specific value of their digital likeness rather than being subjected to a blanket surrender of their livelihood.
[P1.5] Protecting performers in this way does not stifle innovation; it creates a sustainable, ethical environment where creators and studios can collaborate. True consent requires transparency, and without an opt-in standard, the digital revolution in film will be built on the exploitation of those who make it possible.
