[P1.1] From the first lullaby to the last bedtime story, the rituals of early childhood are the scaffolding of a human life. [P1.3] Studies of early language development consistently emphasize the importance of responsive, contingent dialogue—something even the most advanced chatbot cannot authentically provide.
[P1.4] Bedtime routines, too, are about more than efficiency. The physical closeness, the consistent presence, the soft voice that says 'I am here'—these build secure attachment. [P1.5] Moreover, routine outsourcing risks undermining the parent-child relationship itself. [P1.6] The convenience is seductive, but the cost is too high. Parents should remain the primary source of engagement in these formative routines, ensuring that technology assists rather than replaces the human connection that is essential for healthy development.
