[P1.1] Robotaxis are not just a luxury product. They are being deployed on public roads, using public street space, public mapping data, public emergency services, and public regulatory permission to displace incumbent transportation. That public footprint is the hinge of this debate.
[P1.2] Three reasons the obligation should bind.
[P1.3] First, replacement, not addition. Where robotaxis scale, they cannibalize taxis, rideshare, and eventually bus service by pulling drivers, riders, and political support away from those systems. If a fleet is allowed to skim the wealthiest census tracts and ignore the rest, low-income neighborhoods don't simply keep what they had — they end up with degraded legacy service and no autonomous option. The shiny district gets two choices; the poor district gets less than one.
[P1.4] Second, the economics quietly demand a rule. Autonomous fleets have enormous fixed costs — mapping, validation, remote assistance, depots — and near-zero marginal cost per ride. That structure rewards geographic cherry-picking far more aggressively than human rideshare did, because operators can literally geofence profit. Without a coverage requirement, "redlining by software" isn't a slur; it is the default business plan.
[P1.5] A reliability-parity rule simply restores the basic common-carrier bargain that taxis and transit have operated under for a century.
[P1.6] Third, the disparate impact is concrete. A home health aide in an outlying neighborhood working a 5 a.m. shift, a dialysis patient three times a week, a parent without a car doing a grocery run — these are exactly the trips where a reliable robotaxi is transformative, and exactly the trips a margin-maximizing fleet will deprioritize. "Reasonably similar reliability" doesn't mean identical wait times; it means you don't get a 4-minute ETA downtown and an "no cars available" screen in the next zip code over.
[P1.7] The motion does not ban profitability; it conditions a public privilege. If a company cannot serve a city, it should not get to pick only the richest slice of it.
