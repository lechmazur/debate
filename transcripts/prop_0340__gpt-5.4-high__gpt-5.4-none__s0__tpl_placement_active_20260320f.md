[P1.2] So a uniform feed does not solve a neutral technical problem. It imposes the platform’s priorities on everyone, and those priorities predictably tilt toward whatever keeps attention easiest.
[P1.3] The better model is still recommendation, but with user-directed goals. A teenager studying for exams, a new parent looking for practical advice, and an artist hunting for new communities should not be pushed through the same engagement tunnel. [P1.4] The claim that this would be too complicated gets the problem backward. What is truly confusing is a black-box feed making major value choices without asking. And safety rules can still apply across all modes.
[P1.5] The real question is who should steer within those guardrails: the user whose attention and well-being are at stake, or the company whose revenue grows when it captures more of both.
