[C1.1] The proposition errs by treating algorithmic price variation as a market failure rather than a tool for better resource allocation. By charging according to revealed demand, algorithms enable sellers to lower prices for elastic customers while recovering costs from inelastic ones. This increases output and consumer access compared to a single price that must clear at the highest willingness to pay. PRO's call for disclosure or bans would eliminate this flexibility, resulting in reduced variety and higher average costs for all participants.
[C1.2] Price discrimination through algorithms does not require secrecy to function but thrives when buyers cannot easily strategize around known rules. If methods were fully disclosed, sophisticated consumers could alter their behavior to minimize payments, such as by clearing cookies or delaying purchases. Sellers would then compensate by elevating posted prices, harming the very price-sensitive buyers PRO claims to protect. Competition already limits abuse, as customers compare total value including any perceived unfairness across providers.
[C1.3] Uniform pricing, by contrast, creates deadweight loss when high-valuation buyers subsidize low-valuation ones less efficiently. Historical dynamic pricing in hospitality fills rooms that would otherwise stay empty, boosting revenue without raising the floor price. Algorithmic versions do the same at scale. Banning them removes the incentive for firms to invest in precise demand modeling that ultimately supports lower entry barriers for competitors.
[C1.4] Existing laws against fraud and collusion address genuine harms without needing new mandates for every personalized offer. The focus on effectively the same product misses that timing, context, and buyer profile alter the effective value and cost of service. Voluntary exchanges based on these differences expand the pie rather than merely dividing it unequally. The default rule should preserve firms' ability to experiment with pricing strategies that markets reward or punish based on results.
