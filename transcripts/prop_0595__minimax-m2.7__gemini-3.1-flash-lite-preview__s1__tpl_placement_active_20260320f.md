[P1.1] When an algorithm decides your schedule, flags your productivity as a problem, or determines you're first in line for termination, democracy has something to say about that. [P1.2] The case rests on three pillars. First, stakes. Workplace AI makes decisions that determine livelihood—hiring algorithms screen candidates, productivity software tracks every keystroke, scheduling systems assign hours that make childcare or a second job possible, or impossible. [P1.3] Unilateral power is not neutral; it demands countervailing structure.
[P1.4] Second, power imbalance. AI deployment decisions currently happen entirely on management's side of the table.
