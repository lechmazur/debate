[P1.1] When massive investment funds treat farmland as just another asset class, we strip agriculture of its purpose: feeding people and sustaining communities. When institutional investors—hedge funds, private equity, pension funds—acquire vast swathes of farmland, their primary fiduciary duty is to financial returns, not stable food production. [P1.3] The result is a food system increasingly vulnerable to shocks. These actors respond to market signals, not to national need; during a price spike, they have every incentive to export or withhold, not to ensure domestic supply. Our food security becomes hostage to portfolio management.
[P1.4] Second, rural communities. Absentee institutional ownership drains the life from these areas. Profits are extracted to distant headquarters, not reinvested locally. [P1.6] This cap is a targeted defense. It doesn’t stop all investment, but it prevents a future where our most critical resource—the land itself—is controlled by entities whose bottom line has no room for resilience or community.
