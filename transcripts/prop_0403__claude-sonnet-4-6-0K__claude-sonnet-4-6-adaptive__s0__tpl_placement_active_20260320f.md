Once we accept that integration outcomes are comparable between family-stream and economic-stream migrants, the only remaining question is which selection criterion better serves the values a just migration system should reflect. The answer is family reunification—and here is why.
[P1.2] First, the human rights foundation. Family unity is not a policy preference; it is a recognized principle in international human rights frameworks and in the domestic law of virtually every liberal democracy. Economic selection, by contrast, treats people as inputs to a labor market. When outcomes are equivalent, choosing the purely economic model means sacrificing a fundamental human value for no measurable gain.
[P1.3] That is an indefensible trade-off.
[P1.4] Second, integration is not merely a statistical artifact—it is lived experience. Children who grow up with both parents present, spouses who can share a household, elderly relatives who avoid isolation: these relational conditions are precisely what enable people to build roots, learn languages, find work, and contribute. Family networks provide informal support that the state cannot replicate efficiently. The comparable-outcomes condition in the motion may partly *reflect* this: family-reunified migrants often integrate well *because* family is there.
[P1.5] Stripping that mechanism away and replacing it with credential-sorting does not obviously do better, and may conceal the role family structure plays in producing the very outcomes economists credit to skill selection.
[P1.6] Third, consider distributional justice. Skill-based points systems systematically favor applicants from wealthier countries who had access to elite education. Family reunification creates pathways for migrants from a much broader range of backgrounds. When outcomes are similar, a system that concentrates opportunity among the already-advantaged is harder to defend.
[P1.7] The CON position will likely argue that economic selection is more efficient or more predictable. But under the motion's own condition—comparable outcomes—efficiency gains evaporate. What remains is a values question, and on that question, family wins.
