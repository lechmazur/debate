[C1.2] First, distinguish the two uses the motion glues together. In team-building, the goal isn’t prediction—it’s giving colleagues a shared vocabulary to talk about preferences, conflict styles, and communication. A workshop where someone says “I’m a high-D, I jump to decisions; you’re a high-S, you want consensus—let’s meet in the middle” is doing useful work even if the underlying typology is rough. You don’t need predictive validity to run a productive conversation; you need a common language.
[C1.3] Banning the tool because it doesn’t forecast quarterly performance is like banning icebreakers because they don’t predict promotions.
[C1.4] Second, in hiring, the relevant question isn’t “Is the test perfect?” but “Is it better than the alternatives it would replace, used the way responsible employers use it?” Unstructured interviews—the default fallback—are notoriously weak and notoriously biased. If you strip out a structured personality instrument, you don’t get science; you get gut feel, which is demonstrably worse on exactly the discrimination grounds PRO invokes. The honest comparison is tool-versus-tool, not tool-versus-ideal.
[C1.5] Third, the predictive-validity attack is overstated. The Big Five traits underneath these instruments—conscientiousness in particular—have decades of evidence linking them to job performance. DISC and even MBTI capture overlapping variance, imperfectly. “Imperfect” is not “zero,” and a small valid signal, combined with structured interviews and work samples, improves decisions at the margin. Marginal improvements at hiring scale are enormous.
[C1.6] Fourth, the discrimination argument cuts the other way. A documented, consistent instrument creates an audit trail. Replacing it with manager intuition removes the paper trail that lets bias be detected and challenged.
[C1.7] The right answer isn’t prohibition; it’s proportionate use—weighting these tools lightly, never as sole gatekeepers, and pairing them with stronger predictors. PRO’s ban throws out a useful, auditable input and replaces it with something worse.
