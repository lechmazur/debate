[P1.2] Without alternatives, bans accomplish nothing but displacement. When crews clear a sidewalk and offer nowhere to go, residents move three blocks over. [P1.4] The “genuinely available” requirement changes the mechanism entirely. [P1.5] Opposition will appeal to immediate order and neighborhood safety. But order built on displacement is an illusion. It trades a visible encampment for scattered, more vulnerable individuals and higher long-term costs. Justification requires a solution, not a shuffle.
[P1.6] If a city cannot fund the basic infrastructure that makes compliance possible, it has no practical or moral ground to penalize those with nowhere else to survive.
