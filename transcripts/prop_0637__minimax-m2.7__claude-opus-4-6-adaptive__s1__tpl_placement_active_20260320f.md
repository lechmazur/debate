By-right permitting forecloses that leverage entirely.

Displacement of existing low-income residents near transit happens in years, sometimes months.
[C1.5] Accepting short-term displacement in exchange for long-term supply gains asks the most vulnerable residents to absorb certain, immediate harm so that hypothetical future residents receive diffuse, delayed benefits. That is not a trade municipalities should be mandated to accept.
[C1.6] The comparison to Minneapolis and Auckland proves less than PRO suggests. Both cases involve broader zoning reform paired with tenant protections and public investment—not unadorned by-right permitting. Citing them as vindication of the motion as written is selective.
