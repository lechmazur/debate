[P1.1] The core choice facing climate-vulnerable cities is simple: plan orderly, mandatory long-term relocation now, when we have time to minimize harm and cost, or wait for repeated disasters to force chaotic, deadly, far more expensive displacement later.
[P1.2] Proactive mandatory planning saves lives and protects the most vulnerable community members. Voluntary relocation programs consistently leave low-income, disabled, and elderly households trapped, as they lack the savings to move on their own. Phased, mandatory planning lets cities prioritize these groups first, move entire neighborhood blocks together to preserve social ties, arrange for affordable housing and job placements in receiving areas, and avoid the overnight displacement that leaves families homeless after hurricanes, wildfires, or chronic flood events. We have already seen the cost of delay: after repeated flooding in eastern Kentucky in 2022, hundreds of low-income families were displaced with no warning, scattered across three states with no access to their former support systems or regular medical care.
[P1.3] This planning is also drastically cheaper for taxpayers and local governments. Repeated disaster recovery, rebuilding the same infrastructure and homes in high-risk zones year after year, costs multiple times more than pre-planned relocation. Rebuilding the same flood-prone sections of coastal Mississippi after every hurricane over the last 30 years has cost taxpayers roughly 8 times what planned relocation of those communities would have cost two decades ago. Mandatory planning also eliminates the moral hazard of subsidizing repeated rebuilds in zones that will only face worse disasters as climate change accelerates.
