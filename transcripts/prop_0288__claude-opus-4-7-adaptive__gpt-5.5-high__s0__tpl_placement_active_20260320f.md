[P1.1] When the state takes something away from you — your unemployment check, your student visa, your right not to be branded a fraudster — that is an exercise of coercive power. The proposition is simply that this kind of power should not be triggered by a probability score alone. A human who can be questioned, overruled, and held accountable must stand between the model and the citizen.
[P1.2] Three reasons make this the only defensible rule.
[P1.3] First, error rates that look small in aggregate are catastrophic at the individual level. The Dutch childcare benefits scandal pushed tens of thousands of families — disproportionately immigrants — into debt and family separation because an algorithmic risk system was treated as ground truth. Michigan's MiDAS system falsely accused tens of thousands of unemployment fraud. These are not hypotheticals about future AI; they are what already happens when humans rubber-stamp a model.
[P1.4] Second, AI risk models are structurally unsuited to be the final word. They are trained on historical enforcement data, which encodes prior bias; they output correlations, not adjudicated facts; and they cannot hear the explanation that would dissolve the suspicion — the missing document, the name collision, the data-entry error. Due process exists precisely because suspicion is cheap and consequences are expensive. A flag is a reason to look; it is not a finding.
[P1.5] Third, accountability collapses without a human decision-maker. If an algorithm suspends your visa, who do you appeal to? The vendor cites trade secrets, the agency cites the model, the model cites the data. Meaningful human review is what keeps the chain of responsibility intact and keeps judicial review possible at all.
[P1.6] Note how modest the proposition is. It does not ban AI triage, prioritization, or investigation support. It says: before the government cuts off the food assistance, revokes the visa, or labels someone a fraudster, a competent human must actually look. If the CON side cannot defend automated punishment without a human in the loop, they cannot defeat this motion.
