[P1.3] Mandatory interoperability shatters lock-in, forcing platforms to compete on quality rather than captivity. The user wins: better features, better moderation, better privacy, because leaving becomes credible.
[P1.4] History proves this model works. Telecom regulators forced number portability and network interconnection; the phone system thrived, not collapsed. [P1.5] Social messaging is functionally identical: communication networks that derive value from connection, not enclosure.
[P1.6] The proposition's language is precise: access goes to "qualified third-party services." This isn't a mandate to hand data to bad actors. [P1.7] What opponents must explain is why social media alone deserves exemption from the portability principles we apply to banking, telecommunications, and healthcare. Interoperability creates exactly the accountability mechanism that concentrates power destroys.
[P1.8] The stakes are simple: either we accept permanent feudal digital fiefdoms, or we enforce the same portability standards that govern every other essential network.
