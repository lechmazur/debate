[P1.1] Critical minerals are the foundational bedrock of modern military power and economic security. From the rare earth elements essential for advanced fighter jets and guided munitions to the lithium and cobalt driving the global energy transition, access to these resources dictates a nation’s strategic autonomy. When firms closely tied to rival states acquire these critical-mineral assets, they are not merely engaging in market competition; they are executing state-directed strategies to monopolize the supply chains of the future. National-security reviews must presumptively block these acquisitions because the alternative is willingly handing geopolitical adversaries a chokehold over our survival.
[P1.2] First, rival states actively weaponize supply chain dominance. We have repeatedly seen adversaries deploy export controls on essential minerals—such as gallium, germanium, and graphite—to retaliate against diplomatic friction and handicap competing defense industrial bases. Allowing state-backed firms to buy up remaining global critical-mineral assets deepens a dangerous dependency. It gives rival governments the unilateral power to sever access to vital materials exactly when they are needed most: during an international crisis or conflict.
[P1.3] Second, a presumptive block is the only viable regulatory posture because traditional mitigation fails against state-directed actors. Standard national security agreements—such as behavioral mitigation, firewalls, or compliance monitoring—rely on corporate transparency and good faith. Firms closely tied to rival states operate as extensions of their host governments' strategic apparatus, prioritizing geopolitical directives over regulatory compliance. In a crisis, no paper agreement will stop a state-linked firm from diverting supply, halting production, or sharing proprietary extraction technologies with its home government.
[P1.4] By establishing a presumption of denial, we flip the burden of proof. Instead of forcing regulators to uncover hidden state ties or predict future sabotage in a complex, opaque market, the baseline becomes the absolute protection of national security. Anything less treats the very components of our defense infrastructure as just another commodity to be sold to the highest bidder, even if that bidder is our adversary.
