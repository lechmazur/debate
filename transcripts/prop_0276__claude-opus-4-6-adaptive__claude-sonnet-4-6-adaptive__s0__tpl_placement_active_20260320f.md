[C1.3] The EU's EC 261—PRO's own model—has spawned fifteen years of litigation over exactly these edge cases, with national courts reaching contradictory rulings. [C1.4] **Automatic payouts invert incentives perversely.** If compensation is unavoidable the moment a delay crosses a threshold, airlines gain nothing from proactive disclosure. The rational response is to delay the announcement, roll the dice on a last-minute fix, and hope the flight launches—exactly the opposite of passenger welfare.
[C1.5] **Costs cascade to passengers themselves.** Airlines operate on thin margins; mandated payouts don't vanish into corporate profits—they surface as higher base fares, reduced route frequency to smaller markets, and ancillary fee creativity.
