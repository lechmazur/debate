[P1.1] Buy-now-pay-later products operate on the exact same core function as credit cards: they let consumers take possession of goods immediately in exchange for a promise to pay in installments over time, with financial penalties for missed payments that can include steep late fees and long-term credit score damage. That makes them credit products, not trivial checkout add-ons, and they deserve the same guardrails that have protected credit card users for decades. Right now, most BNPL providers face almost none of the rules that govern credit cards.
[P1.2] A college student buying an $80 textbook can sign up for a BNPL plan in one click, no ability-to-repay check required, miss a $20 payment because they forgot the due date was not aligned with their pay schedule, and get hit with a $15 late fee plus a negative mark on their credit report that stays for seven years. If that same student used a credit card for the purchase, they would have a mandatory 21-day grace period for payments, a late fee capped at an amount no higher than their minimum payment, clear upfront disclosure of all penalty terms before they signed up, and the right to dispute errors on their credit report directly with the issuer.
[P1.3] Claims that BNPL is not credit because most plans are interest-free ignore that credit cards routinely offer 0% intro APR periods, and that classification never changes based on temporary interest waivers. Regulating BNPL like credit cards does not eliminate the product’s convenience for users who pay on time; it simply closes the regulatory loophole that lets providers expose millions of consumers to avoidable, devastating financial harm for trivial missteps.

Credit-card rules were written for a different era of revolving plastic debt, not today's targeted installment checkout.
[C1.4] Market discipline already pressures BNPL providers to tighten practices faster than regulators can legislate. Heavy credit-style mandates would raise merchant costs, reduce availability, and ultimately shrink options for responsible consumers who value the simplicity.
