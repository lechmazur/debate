[P1.3] A mindfulness app cannot compensate for a calendar that never stops. Recovery requires genuine absence from work, not another push notification on a locked screen.
[P1.4] In-person gatherings rebuild the social fabric that fragmented work frays. Human connection is not a software feature you download; it is a shared environment you must actively fund.
[P1.5] The weighing is straightforward. Wellness apps are cheap, scalable, and largely performative. They shift responsibility onto employees while leaving burnout’s structural drivers untouched. Reallocation is not about abandoning digital tools entirely, but about recognizing that an app subscription cannot buy back exhausted hours or manufacture belonging.
[P1.6] Predictable time off and in-person gathering cost more upfront, but directly remove the conditions creating the problem.
