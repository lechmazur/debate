[P1.2] The debt-to-GDP ratio, already above 120%, approaches levels historically associated with fiscal crises in other advanced economies, where high debt has led to slower growth, financial instability, and in some cases, sudden loss of market access. Without corrective action, rising debt could trigger higher borrowing costs across the economy, hurting businesses and families, dampening private investment, and making it harder for the government to invest in priorities.

Deficit reduction is not about austerity for its own sake—it is about preserving the capacity to govern effectively and protecting economic stability. The status quo is unsustainable, and the longer we delay, the more painful the adjustment will be.
[P1.4] Prioritizing deficit reduction now is the responsible choice to avoid a future where interest payments dominate the budget, economic growth is stifled, and future generations inherit an unsustainable burden of debt. [C1.5] "Serious deficit reduction" as an overriding priority means Social Security cuts, Medicare cuts, infrastructure freezes.
