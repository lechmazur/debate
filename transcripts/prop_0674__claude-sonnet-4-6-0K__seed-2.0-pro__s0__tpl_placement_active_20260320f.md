[P1.1] The central question here is simple: what tells an expansionist power to stop? The answer deterrence theory has offered for decades is cost. When aggression is made prohibitively expensive, it slows or stops. When it succeeds cheaply, it continues.
[P1.2] That logic is not abstract in this case — it has already been tested. After Russia invaded Georgia in 2008 with minimal Western consequence, it seized Crimea in 2014. After Crimea was absorbed at low cost and Western sanctions proved manageable, Russia launched a full-scale invasion in 2022. The pattern is not coincidental.
[P1.3] Each episode of inadequate Western response lowered the perceived cost of the next move. Sustained military aid to Ukraine breaks that pattern by making the costs concrete and ongoing.
[P1.4] An immediate negotiated settlement would do the opposite. Whatever territorial or political concessions such a settlement required would effectively ratify the outcome of force. Russia would retain gains it could not hold without fighting. Every neighboring state — and every other would-be revisionist — would observe that a nuclear-armed power can revise borders by military force and then bargain from those gains.
[P1.5] The deterrent signal is not just to Russia; it reaches Beijing, Pyongyang, and beyond.
[P1.6] CON will likely argue that continued war risks escalation and that diplomacy reduces danger. But this conflates de-escalation with deterrence. A premature settlement freezes rather than resolves the conflict, leaving Russia rearmed, territorially rewarded, and positioned to resume pressure — on Ukraine or on NATO's eastern members — when the moment suits it. The Baltic states and Poland are not being paranoid when they argue that Ukraine's resistance is also their defense.
[P1.7] Military aid, then, is not merely a choice about Ukraine. It is the mechanism through which Western resolve is made legible to the aggressor. Credible resistance now is the most direct path to preventing a wider war later. That is the case this debate should revolve around.
