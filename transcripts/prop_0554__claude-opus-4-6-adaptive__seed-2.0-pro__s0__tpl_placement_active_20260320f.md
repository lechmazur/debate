[P1.1] Every other form of healthcare recognizes that automated screening must eventually yield to professional judgment. A pulse oximeter triggers a nurse call. An abnormal lab flags a physician review. Mental-health AI should be no different—and the stakes of getting it wrong are lethal.
[P1.2] The core of this case rests on three claims.
[P1.3] **First, AI chatbots hit a therapeutic ceiling that patients cannot reliably detect.** Someone in psychological distress is, almost by definition, impaired in their ability to evaluate whether treatment is working. Chatbots can deliver structured exercises—cognitive-behavioral prompts, mindfulness scripts—but they cannot perform differential diagnosis, detect medication interactions, recognize emerging psychosis, or sense the non-verbal cues that experienced clinicians use to avert crises. Without a mandatory handoff, patients who need more stay trapped below the ceiling indefinitely, mistaking activity for progress.
[P1.4] **Second, market incentives actively resist voluntary handoff.** AI therapy platforms monetize engagement. Every session a user stays inside the app is revenue retained; every referral to a human provider is revenue lost. Expecting these companies to self-regulate referral timing is like expecting a casino to remind you to leave. A fixed-session legal mandate removes the perverse incentive and creates a bright-line standard regulators can enforce.
[P1.5] **Third, a fixed-session rule is the least restrictive safeguard that actually works.** Alternatives—like requiring handoff only when risk is detected—depend on the very AI whose limitations we are worried about. A time-based trigger is simple, auditable, and technology-neutral. It does not ban AI therapy; it bookends it. Patients can return to the chatbot afterward if a human clinician agrees the tool remains appropriate.
[P1.6] The mandate simply ensures that at least once, a qualified professional lays eyes on the case.
[P1.7] The population using AI mental-health tools skews toward those who already face barriers to care: cost, stigma, geographic isolation. These users deserve more protection, not less. A mandatory handoff converts AI therapy from an unmonitored endpoint into a genuine first step in a care pathway.
