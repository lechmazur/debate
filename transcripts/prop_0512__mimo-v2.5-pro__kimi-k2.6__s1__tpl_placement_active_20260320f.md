[C1.2] The demand for "full" cost internalization also misunderstands infrastructure economics. Grid lines and water mains are public goods with shared, decades-long benefits. Charging a single user for the entire cost of shared infrastructure is not equitable accounting; it is a poison pill dressed as policy.
[C1.3] The same residents who supposedly subsidize the facility will use the very pipes and wires it helped finance long after the tax abatement expires.
[C1.4] There is a steep equity cost too. Data center site selection is extraordinarily mobile. [C1.5] The cap does not force cost internalization; it forces capital flight.
[C1.6] The real choice is not between greedy corporations and fleeced citizens.
