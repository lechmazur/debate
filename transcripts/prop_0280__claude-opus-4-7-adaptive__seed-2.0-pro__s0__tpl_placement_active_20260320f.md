[P1.1] The modern wedding is a strange economic creature: a single day that now routinely costs as much as a car, a tuition year, or a down payment. And the cost isn't just money. It's months of logistics, family politics, Instagram choreography, and the slow conversion of a private commitment into a public performance graded by strangers.
[P1.2] The proposition doesn't ask couples to burn tradition. It asks whether they should feel *obligated* to follow it. My answer is no, and the reason is simple: an obligation only deserves weight when the thing it protects still exists. What "tradition" delivers today is largely not the meaning people imagine.
[P1.3] The white dress, the engagement ring priced at some multiple of monthly salary, the destination bachelorette, the tiered reception, the curated hashtag — most of these are not ancient rites. They are twentieth-century marketing victories. De Beers invented the diamond standard. Bridal magazines invented the checklist.
[P1.4] Social media invented the audience. Calling this "tradition" launders commerce as heritage.
[P1.5] Once you see that, the obligation collapses into three real harms. First, financial: couples routinely start marriage in debt or drain savings to perform a day, and money stress is among the top predictors of early marital conflict. Second, attentional: the performance crowds out the relationship. Planning a spectacle for 150 guests is not the same as preparing for a life with one person.
[P1.6] Third, coercive: the "expectations" are enforced by relatives, vendors, and feeds, not by the couple's own values. An obligation imposed from outside, serving industries and optics, is exactly the kind that deserves little weight.
[P1.7] The alternative isn't nihilism. It's proportion. A courthouse ceremony, a backyard dinner, a small ritual that actually means something to the two people involved — these can carry more weight than a $40,000 production. The proposition simply says: couples get to choose.
[P1.8] Tradition should have to earn its claim on them, not collect it by default.
