[P1.2] Nationality is morally arbitrary. None of us chose the country we were born into. [P1.3] The mere fact of shared citizenship is not a deep moral fact; it is a bureaucratic one.
[P1.4] This does not mean national attachments are worthless. They function as practical coordination mechanisms—states can deliver public goods efficiently, and local obligations have real value. [P1.6] The opposing view leads somewhere damaging. [P1.7] History confirms the danger. The modern era's worst moral catastrophes—genocide, colonial extraction, ethnic cleansing—were executed under banners of national and communal loyalty, not as a corruption of that logic but as a direct expression of it.
