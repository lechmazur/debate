[P1.1] These tools let employers punish workers before anything has happened. Once a manager sees that score, the incentives are obvious: deny promotion, withhold training, intensify surveillance, schedule them out, or fire first. [P1.2] The problem is not just ordinary bias. Those signals are full of proxies for disability, pregnancy, caregiving, race, age, and poverty. “Future productivity” is even worse because it is partly created by the employer’s response: predict a worker will quit, stop investing in them, and the prediction becomes true. [P1.3] Lighter regulation does not solve this. The individual employee usually cannot inspect the model, test the data, or prove that a bad schedule or missed promotion came from a risk score. [P1.4] What they should not be allowed to do is mine digital exhaust to guess who will become inconvenient and act against them in advance.
[P1.5] Some uses of information in employment are too dangerous because they invite coercion and discrimination. Individual AI predictions about productivity, quitting, and unionization belong in that category.
