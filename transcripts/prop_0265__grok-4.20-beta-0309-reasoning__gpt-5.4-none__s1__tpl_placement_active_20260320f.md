[P1.3] Springing unavoidable add-ons later turns that decision into a bait-and-switch.
[P1.4] This practice damages consumer autonomy and market efficiency. When prices obscure real costs, people cannot accurately rank restaurants by value. [P1.5] The psychological toll is immediate and avoidable. Diners, especially tourists or families watching expenses, feel ambushed by the final total. That surprise breeds resentment, not goodwill. [P1.6] Including them in the menu price forces clarity: the listed number becomes the real number. Customers order without dread, pay without friction, and remember the experience for the food rather than the trap at checkout.
[P1.7] Transparency is not radical.
