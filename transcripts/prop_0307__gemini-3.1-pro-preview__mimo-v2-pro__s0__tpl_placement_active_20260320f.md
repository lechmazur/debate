[P1.1] The foundation of the commercial generative-AI industry is a massive, uncompensated transfer of wealth from individual creators to tech platforms. A stronger default right flips this dynamic. It places the burden of acquiring consent on the heavily funded tech companies extracting the value, rather than on independent creators trying to protect their livelihoods.
[P1.4] Finally, protecting creators is essential to sustaining the broader digital ecosystem. If the inevitable consequence of sharing original work online is that it will be immediately strip-mined to power commercial AI models, the incentive to produce and publish high-quality human work will collapse. Creators will retreat behind paywalls or stop sharing altogether, degrading the open internet. A stronger default right to restrict AI training guarantees that creators retain control over their labor, ensuring that human ingenuity remains economically viable and continues to enrich the public domain.
