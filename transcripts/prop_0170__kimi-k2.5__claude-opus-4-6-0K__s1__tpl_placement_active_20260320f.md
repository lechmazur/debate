[P1.1] Water scarcity is not a crisis of demand but of imagination. [P1.2] Conservation and rationing inevitably collide with hard ceilings. You cannot conserve water that does not exist during a decade-long drought, nor can you ration your way to industrial growth. A desalination plant provides guaranteed cubic meters; a public awareness campaign provides hope.
[P1.4] Finally, reliance on rationing institutionalizes inequality. Wealthy residents maintain lawns through greywater loopholes while the poor queue for hours at public taps. Desalination democratizes access through piped infrastructure, treating water as a public utility to be generated, not a private privilege to be hoarded.
[P1.5] Regions facing structural scarcity deserve more than managed decline. They deserve the capacity to grow.
